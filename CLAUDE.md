# CLAUDE.md

Guidance for Claude and other agents working in `awesome-ai-for-bioengineering`.

## Purpose

This repository is a curated Awesome List tracking AI engineering resources relevant to bioengineering: biological foundation models, protein design, molecular generation, simulation, autonomous laboratories, datasets, benchmarks, tooling, and responsible release practices.

It is a technical index, not a research wiki, documentation site, or laboratory resource.

## Content rules

- `README.md` is the primary and only content artefact. All curated resources live there.
- Do not create separate content directories (`docs/`, `papers/`, `tools/`, `datasets/`, `benchmarks/`, `notes/`, `evaluation/`, `labs-and-companies/`, `templates/`).
- Do not create category files outside `README.md`.
- Do not add research notes, weekly digests, or signal logs.
- Support files are limited to curation, contribution, review, CI, link quality, and agent operation.

## Curation standard

- Follow Awesome List conventions: curated, not exhaustive; quality over volume.
- Keep entries to one line in the form `* [Name](link) - Neutral, technically precise description.`
- Prefer canonical sources: GitHub repositories, papers (arXiv/DOI), official documentation, institutional or project pages.
- Keep descriptions neutral. Avoid hype, superlatives, and unverifiable claims.
- Maintain alphabetical order within sections where practical.
- No duplicate entries and no dead links.
- Do not add low-quality resources to fill out a section.

## Safety boundaries

- This is not a clinical, medical, laboratory, or experimental-protocol repository.
- Do not include wet-lab protocols, pathogen engineering guidance, genetic-manipulation procedures, synthesis routes, or any operational biological experimentation detail.
- Keep entries at the level of AI systems and what they model, predict, or generate.
- Responsible release and biosecurity resources are in scope at a high, governance-oriented level only.

## Working rules

- Do not rewrite the thesis, scope, or section structure unless explicitly asked.
- Make small, reviewable changes.
- After editing `README.md`, run or recommend the CI checks (awesome-lint, link check, repository-health).
